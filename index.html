About Me

Nurse by night, programmer by day. I always had a interest in technology, but my parents wanted me to pursue a career in the medical field. I became a nurse in 2016 and have been working in various fields - medical/surgical, pediatrics home health services, and psycholoyy. I like helping people but I couldn't find a field that I was truely passionate. In 2017, I decided to pick up coding as a hobby and I started learning coding on my own using sites such as pluralsight, codeschool, freecodecamp, w3school, and other resources. The more time I spent learning, the more I realized how interesting coding was. I decided to join a UT coding bootcamp in April 2018 to accelerate my learning and that was when I decided to finally fully transition into becoming a software developer.

I did my prerequisites at Lone Star community college and University of Houston main campus. I received an assoicate of science at Lone Star before going to UT health science center - School of Nursing in 2015. Graduated in 2016 with a bachelor of science in nursing and I have been working as a nurse ever since.

Relevant technology: ASP.NET/C#, Javascript, HTML5, CSS, JQuery, SQL, LINQ, NodeJS, Angular
